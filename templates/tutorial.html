Using Seating50

A Quick Overview

A student's primary source of distraction is other students, which is why a well-designed seating plan is essential for classroom productivity. Creating a perfect plan, however, is much easier said than done.

Fortunately, I think this problem is based on some fairly simple conditions. That is, some students must be separated, while others should be combined. Others too, may need to be placed nearer the front of the classroom. That's it!

Seating50 will take you through three easy steps in which you will enter your preferences that pertain to these conditions. The ultimate quality of the outputted plan depends solely on the quantity of inputted information. I thus encourage you to challenge the algorithm!

Step 1: Presetting

Seating50 is not currently designed for all possible classroom layouts, but two popular options will be available with a range of dimensions. On the first page, you will chose your preferred of such desk arrangements. Use the buttons on the bottom right side of the page to toggle height, width, and Partnered Desk Mode. Note: If you chose partnered desks, each student will be limited to a single compatible partner.

Next, you must input all student names. Try to separate names with new lines, spaces, commas, and/or semi-colons. If there are duplicate names, add a dot followed by some initial of the last name to distinguish these. The algorithm will place these names on the grid for the final plan, so we must be able to know who is who!

Step 2: Chosing Pairs

Now, your student names will be displayed as a grid of buttons. You must chose as many incompatible and compatible student pairs as you can imagine. Use the "+" buttons to change the input category. Seating50 will try to position compatible pairs beside one another in the final plan, so these should be students who work well together. On the other hand, incompatible pairs represent those who are unproductive or obstructive, and will thus be separated from eachother.

Tip: When selecting incompatible pairs, you may double click on a student to activate Isolate Mode. This student will be permanently selected, and any subsequent selections will be added as an incompatible pair with this student. Isolate Mode was made for the more problematic students who should ideally be separated from several classmates.

Step 3: Fronts and Highly Incompatibles

As a bonus, Seating50 allows users to input two extra smaller lists to be considered by the algorithm. First, you may chose some students to be positioned nearer the front of the class. The maximum amount of these students depends on the width of the classroom. These students will still be placed with their compatible and incompatible partners (if possible), but they will be located in the first two rows of the seating plan.

Next, you may chose up to two highly incompatible student pairs. The algorithm will try to separate these students on opposite margins of the classroom. There is no requirement for you to chose such pairs, but maximal separation between some students can certainly be helpful in some cases.

Enjoy Your Plan!

You should be presented with a seating plan that meets all of your needs. Highly incompatible students will be highlighted in red, and the front-bound student names will be orange.

By hovering over the tables, you can see how all your student pairs were ultimately positioned. If any pairs were omitted due to logical impossibilities, you will be notified at the bottom of the page. There, you will also find some buttons that allow you to regenerate, restart or change your inputs if needed. This was Seating50!
